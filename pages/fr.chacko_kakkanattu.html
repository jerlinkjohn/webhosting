Fr. Chacko Kakkanattu SDB

Kohima, Nagaland

Fr. Chacko left home in 1951 to become a Salesian missionary. He was ordinated on 9th April, 1967 from Most Reverend Dr. Stephan Fernando at Don Bosco, Shillong, Assam. He was destined to spend his vocation for the souls in the North-East of India. Filling himself with rays of eternal sun; Fr. Chacko become the beacon of light to diverse group of peoples. His great deeds did not limit to just evangelization, but reaching out to several isolated communities in the region and imparting eternal light to their lives; socially, economically, politically and most of all, spiritually.

Wishes from Most reverend Provincial and Bishops on his Golden jubilee of priestly ordiation.

Fr. Chacko braved all odds to bring sunshine to peoples living in remote corners of Northeast India. He is apostle of the Mising tribe and his pioneering spirit cannot be constrained by any obstacles
Rev. Fr. Nestor Guria SBD, Provincial
I have known Fr. Chacko from 1953 and his work in Golaghat is greatly remembered. I have toured with him long days during winter and have had several evangelical programs together
+ Rt. Rev. Thomas Menamparampil SBD
Rev. Fr. Chacko is a great Salesian Missionary in the North-East India. He lived a simple and sacrificing life.He has no show, but a great heart for the people inneed, which made him, loved by all
+ Rt. Rev. Joseph Aind SBD, Bishop of Dibrugarh
His days in DibangSapori and the initial years of Pagalam were very hard but he put up with all those so gladly. I thank Fr. Chacko for all that he has been to these missions in East Arunachal and is example will always remain with us all
+ Rt. Rev. George Pallipparambil SBD, Bishop of Miao
His committed apostolic labour, particularly in Assam brought many souls to Christ. His unassuming and gentle ways coupled with his availability and service mindedness drew people to him and made him a great pastor
